DHRDNet

“Defending Human Rights, Delivering Hope.”

Who We Are

DHRDNet is a coalition of more than 1,000 Dalit human-rights defenders across India. Although its work spans many states, the network concentrates especially in the states of Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra and Karnataka

Our Mission & Vision

Their mission is to strengthen the capacity of Dalit human-rights defenders (HRDs), monitor rights violations, and ensure the laws and mechanisms meant to protect Dalits are implemented in practice. They envision a society where caste-based discrimination is systematically challenged and where Dalits have equal access to justice, dignity and opportunity.

Core Focus Areas

Capacity Building:Provide training, resources and support to HRDs (particularly Dalit women) so they can better defend rights at the grassroots.
Legal Assistance & Monitoring: Develop programmes that support access to justice for Dalits, document violations, and push for accountability of institutions.
Advocacy & Public Dialogue: Engage with communities and government bodies to raise awareness, promote Dalit identity, and reinforce enforcement of anti-discrimination laws.

Impact & Milestones

Since their inception, DHRDNet has built a nationwide network of HRDs from diverse backgrounds and states. It has also improved access to justice for large numbers of Dalits by linking grassroots defenders with legal resources and monitoring tools.

Looking Ahead

DHRDNet continues to expand its outreach, deepen its capacity-building efforts, and strengthen the implementation of anti-discrimination laws in India. Their next steps include broader community engagement, stronger public campaigns, and deeper collaboration with states where caste-based violence remains a critical challenge.

What Are Honour Killings?

Understanding the issue is the first step toward ending it

Honour killings are acts of violence, usually murder, committed by family members against individuals — predominantly women and girls — who are perceived to have brought shame or dishonour to the family.

Victims may be targeted for refusing arranged marriages, seeking divorce, engaging in relationships of their own choosing, or simply exercising their basic human rights.

Human Rights Violation

Honour killings violate the right to life, freedom, and safety.

Not About Culture

No culture or religion justifies taking a life in the name of honour.

Education is Key

Awareness and education can help prevent these tragedies.

Important:

If you or someone you know is in danger, please seek help immediately. Contact local authorities or reach out to organizations specializing in protecting victims of honour-based violence.

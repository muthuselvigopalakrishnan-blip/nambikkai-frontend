Dhanak

“Celebrating Love Beyond Boundaries.”

Who We Are

Dhanak (meaning “rainbow”) is a registered non-profit organisation in India led by survivors of natal-family violence and relationship-choice suppression. Founded as a support group in 2004 and formally registered under the Societies Registration Act in 2012, it has evolved into a key advocate for individuals whose autonomy in education, employment, relationships or gender identity is challenged by family- or caste-/religion-based pressures.

Our Mission & Vision

Their mission is to protect the right of every person to choose their life path — including whom to love or marry — free from violence, coercion or discrimination. They envision a society where differences of caste, religion, gender or sexual orientation are not barriers but sources of richness and freedom.

Core Focus Areas

Crisis Intervention & Safe Support: Providing legal, mental-health and safe-shelter assistance to inter-faith, inter-caste, LGBTQIA+ couples and individuals at risk of honour-based or familial violence.
Counselling & Legal Aid: Guiding individuals through relationship-choice issues, facilitating marriage registration under the law, and connecting with protective systems and safe-houses.
Awareness & Advocacy: Challenging discriminatory social norms, educating about constitutional rights, and building inclusive attitudes toward relationships, identity, and life-choices.

Impact & Milestones

Dhanak has directly supported thousands of individuals facing family- or community-based violence due to relationship or identity choices. It is among the few organisations in India to operate a gender-neutral shelter for couples and individuals at risk. The organisation addresses daily multiple queries from people in precarious situations—evidence of its real-time reach and relevance.

Looking Ahead

Dhanak continues to expand its network of legal, mental-health and shelter support, deepen its awareness-building campaigns, and push for stronger institutional and policy responses to honour-based violence, forced marriage and suppressed autonomy. Their future direction includes broader geographic outreach, deeper engagement with adolescent and youth populations, and stronger alliances for systemic change.
